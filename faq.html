Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the summit

Q: Who can attend REAIM 2027?

A: Government delegations (invited), industry representatives, academia, civil society, international organizations, and youth delegates (ages 19-34).

Q: How do I register?

A: Through your country's Designated Accreditation Officer (DAO). Contact registration@reaim2027.mod.go.ke for assistance.

Q: What is the dress code?

A: Business attire (lounge suit) for all sessions. Welcome Reception - business attire.

Q: Are there interpretation services?

A: English, French, and Swahili simultaneous interpretation for livestreamed sessions. Interpretation booths available for Ministerial Roundtable national remarks only (non-English). Delegations must bring own interpreters and notify by the deadline.

Q: Can we hold bilateral meetings?

A: Yes. Book via provided Calendly links. Priority given to Ministerial/Vice-Ministerial level.

Q: What documents will be produced?

A: A 'Blueprint for Action' outcome document will be endorsed at the Closing Session.

Q: Are there youth activities?

A: Yes. Full youth programme including policy proposals, challenge awards, tabletop exercise, and an AI exhibition.
